Browser Compatbility

Methodology

Our browser compatibility policy is based on looking at the analytics for our websites. The general rule of thumb is any browser with less than 0.1% of all sessions probably doesn’t need to be supported. Exceptions to this rule may come up if a browser has relatively low usage, is no longer supported, and is very hard for us to support.

Supported Browsers (last updated April 7th, 2016)

  • Chrome: 40
  • Safari (desktop): 5 (partial)
    • It should look reasonable and function in Safari 5, but doesn’t have to be perfect
    • Safari 6 should be fully functioning and look completely correct
  • Firefox: 38
  • IE: 11 and Edge
    • Support was dropped for IE 8/9/10 when they were no longer supported by Microsoft AND usage dropped bellow 1%
    • Up until relatively recently IE 8 was the most used version of IE by a large margin, thus why it took so long for us to change our policy
  • Opera and Opera Mini
    • Usage of these browsers is low enough that we only support the most modern version
    • Luckily if it works in Chrome, it should work in all the most recent versions of Opera
    • When this was written we had more sessions coming from Opera Mini than Opera
  • Safari for iOS: whatever shipped with iOS 9 and above
    • Most users seem to be keeping up with the latest version of iOS

Further Information

For more information about what you can and can’t use with these browsers check out caniuse.com.
